**Receiving mass amounts of emails every second had me deeply alarmed.**
_P-P-P-PING._ _P-P-P-PING._ My internal panic grew with
the concerning sound of split-second notifications abruptly being
cut off by the next one. One of my accounts was compromised, an
unauthorized purchase was made, and underneath hundreds of junk
emails, laid a purchase receipt. Flooding my inbox with spam was the
hacker’s best attempt at burying the expensive order confirmation
that was sent at the time of sale. Determined to ruin their scam, I
accepted the challenge, took a breath, and focused.

With utmost urgency, my first action was to change the password to
secure my account. Done. Next, I quickly attempted to cancel the
costly order, but unfortunately, I struggled to locate it within my
account’s order history. Sure enough, the hacker cleverly concealed
the order. With certainty it was there, I eventually discovered a
search filter that revealed the hidden order. Now it was time to
cancel the malicious order and go about my day… or so I had hoped.

Upon contacting the sales platform, they assured me that the order
would be cancelled. I soon realized a major problem… the order had
somehow already been shipped to the address submitted by the hacker.
“Already?” I emphasized to myself, worriedly. With a racing mind, I
desperately reached out to the seller to explain the situation and,
once again, requested cancellation. Hoping this would be the end, I
felt a sense of temporary relief. Little did I know that the seller
would not respond to me at all throughout this entire time, despite
updating them numerous times over the week. With the shipment
already on the way, time was running out, and I had to take matters
into my own hands.

“What more can I do?” I thought hopelessly with the feeling that
they would succeed in their scam. I knew that I would likely be
refunded for the order; however, at this point, there was much more
to it than that. It had become a personal mission. Inspired by
online content creators who expose scams, I was determined to win
this package tug-of-war battle that I had found myself in. Having
some experience selling and shipping items myself, I knew there had
to be a way to somehow pause the shipping process in case of an
error. Later, I learned that the carrier offers a package
interception service, which, for a fee, stops a delivery and
redirects it to a local post office for pickup. This fee, of course,
was well worth it to me in my redemption story arc. In fact, I was
so determined that I even paid twice for the service because the
tracking status updated itself to “Preparing for Delivery,” as if
all my efforts were ignored, leaving me concerned. This was the
final stretch, and I was disappointed, thinking to myself that the
package would end up in the scammer’s hands. Being anxiously on the
edge of my seat, the status updated to… “Intercepted”—finally—some
lasting relief leading to the end of a stressful ordeal.

Today, I feel proud for successfully ending the scam and leaving the
malicious attacker empty-handed. In retrospect, this all started as
a result of a company’s data breach which exposed millions of email
addresses, including my own, which effectively placed an eventual
target on my back. One important note is that if I had enabled
two-factor authentication (2FA), it’s likely that none of this would
have happened. Unfortunately, 2FA can be inconvenient, which deters
many people from enabling it, including myself at the time. If
companies, especially those in e-commerce where payments are
involved, forced their users to enable two-factor authentication,
many hacking attempts would be disrupted and stopped dead in their
tracks.

Most importantly, it was a chance to learn from an unfortunate
experience. To create a silver lining, I developed both a stronger
sense of security practices and a deeper passion for Cybersecurity,
which I’m currently pursuing in school. Moreover, I’m in disbelief
at how common company data breaches are, leaving millions of people
potentially vulnerable to a wide range of cyber-attacks like mine.
With a newfound passion for Cybersecurity, I can only hope to
contribute toward a positive impact in the future.
